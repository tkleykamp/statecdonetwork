Why a State CDO Network?

The CDO role is new within state government, with Colorado being the first state to appoint a CDO in 2011 followed by New York (2012), Illinois (2013), then Connecticut (2014). By 2016 there were approximately 10 state CDOs. Currently there are approximately 25 state CDOs or equivalent positions across the country. States are recognizing that data is a strategic asset and they have great potential to unlock it for public good -- for example, to promote a complete and accurate census in 2020, to integrate data across social service agencies in order to serve multi-system youth and reduce homelessness, and to respond more quickly to natural and public health disasters.
